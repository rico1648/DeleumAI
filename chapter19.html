19. Risk Assessment

Use of AI Tools – Disclosure and Review Policy

This policy applies to all employees, interns, contractors, and anyone producing written, analytical, or creative work using AI tools in the course of their duties.

If you use AI tools (such as ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini, or others) to generate content, insights, reports, or drafts, you are required to disclose this to your team or relevant stakeholders. AI-generated content should not be treated as a finished product without review.

To support responsible use of AI:

  • Save and share the prompts used when generating AI content.
  • Allow colleagues to verify the accuracy and integrity of outputs.
  • Use this as an opportunity to support shared learning and responsible innovation.

The goal is to identify and correct factual errors, bias, or hallucinations before the content is used for decision-making or shared externally.

AI Risk Assessment Policy

This policy applies to all staff, contractors, and partners who use AI tools in any part of their work, including content generation, data analysis, decision-making, customer communication, and automation of internal processes.

Before deploying AI-generated content or outputs—especially in public-facing materials, strategic decisions, or critical operations—a basic risk assessment must be conducted. At minimum, consider the following:

  • Accuracy and Reliability: Is the output factually correct and reviewed by a subject matter expert?
  • Bias and Fairness: Could the output reinforce stereotypes or exclude certain groups? Was the input data appropriate?
  • Confidentiality and Data Privacy: Was any sensitive or proprietary information entered into the AI? Are we compliant with data privacy policies?
  • Impact and Reputational Risk: Could the output cause confusion, offense, or harm if shared? Would we be comfortable standing behind it publicly?
  • Transparency and Auditability: Have you disclosed that AI was used, and are the original prompts and outputs saved for audit or review?

Any high-risk AI use (e.g. customer-facing content, compliance reporting, executive briefings) should be reviewed by a manager or designated reviewer before implementation.

This policy supports innovation while protecting the integrity, accuracy, and trustworthiness of our work.

We continuously evaluate the risks associated with AI tool usage:

  • Periodic Assessments: The organization must conduct periodic risk assessments for AI Tools usage, including an evaluation of potential vulnerabilities, threats, and impacts on Deleum's operations, reputation, and compliance.
  • Mitigation: Based on the risk assessment, the organization should implement appropriate security measures and controls to mitigate identified risks and ensure the secure and responsible use of AI Tools.
