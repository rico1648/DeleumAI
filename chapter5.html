4. Protecting Deleum's Data: A Top Priority

This is one of the most critical aspects of using AI tools. We must prevent any accidental disclosure of sensitive or confidential information.

Here's what you MUST do:

  • NEVER input sensitive or confidential information: This includes, but is not limited to, financial data, personal information, intellectual property, trade secrets, or strategic plans.
    • Real-life Example: Do NOT type "Draft an email to our client, XYZ Corp, about our Q3 earnings which are projected to be $5 million, an increase of 15%." This includes financial data. Instead, you could type "Draft a professional email to a client regarding positive quarterly earnings."
  • Review ALL AI-generated content: Before you share anything created by an AI tool, whether internally or externally, double-check it to ensure it doesn't contain or imply any sensitive information.
    • Real-life Example: If AI generates a report summary, read it carefully to ensure it doesn't accidentally reveal specific project timelines or confidential client names if those are sensitive.
  • Report accidental disclosures immediately: If you realize you've accidentally shared sensitive information with an AI tool, report it immediately to the ICT Department or your superior. Don't try to fix it yourself; prompt reporting is key.
